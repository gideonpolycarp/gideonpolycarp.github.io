My Youth My Foundation

  • Written by Gideon Polycarp
  • @Gideon

    • Youthhood is a period of maturity in one's life. The oxford dictionary defines this period as a whole early part of life from childhood. Foundation in the otherhand is the starting point of all things. The foundation or starting point of a thing has a lot to do with its continuation and end. A building built on a solid foundation will definitely remain firm and unmoveable. The same is applicable to life.

    Youthhood is a period when foundations are built. This is the starting point of life, it's a very crucial point because any mistake made at this point has an irreversible effect in times to come. The future being bright or dim, full of happiness or regrets, enjoyable or endurable depends on this point. It's best to struggle now in building a strong foundation for the future and relax later in the future; but it's quite unfortunate that many misunderstand the delicacy of this stage and prefer to enjoy now to endure later. It's so pathetic. The strenght, vibrance, potentials, gifts and talents of a man are active at this stage. You tend to strive for independence and privacy because you're in you activation point. This is when life starts for you and you take decisions all by your self. Mind you, all decisions made today have effects either positively or negatively in your future.

    There are laws and principles that are applicable to everyone inspite of the religious, tribal and national diversities and "sowing and reaping" is one. This law states that " we reap what we sow". You can't sow yam and reap mango. This entails that whatever we do now, we must bear the consequencies either now or in the future. There are questions you should always ask yourself while building your future: Are you making the right decisions? Do you know your future depends on you? Are you aware your generation is waiting for you? Are you getting ready for an endurable or enjoyable future?
